## AI Limitations — Hallucinations and Errors

This project is powered by a large language model (Claude). LLMs can and do produce **hallucinations**, **factual errors**, and **incorrect legal analysis**. Outputs may:

- **Fabricate verification results** — claim a citation was verified as existing when it was not actually checked, or report a citation as nonexistent when it does exist
- **Misclassify citation status** — assign `Verified` to an authority that doesn't support the claimed proposition, or `Nonexistent` to a valid but unfamiliar citation
- **Miss critical issues** — fail to identify hallucinated authorities, logical defects, or substantive errors in the reviewed document
- **Generate false positives** — flag correct citations, sound reasoning, or proper formatting as defective
- **Produce inconsistent scoring** — assign grades or release recommendations that don't match the actual findings
- **Introduce hallucinations in review comments** — make factual claims in margin comments that are themselves unsupported or incorrect
- **Misparse document structure** — fail to correctly extract sections, numbering, cross-references, or defined terms from complex DOCX files

The agent includes built-in safeguards (dual-track citation verification, verification status taxonomy, 7-item self-check quality gate, anti-hallucination mandate), but **these safeguards reduce — not eliminate — the risk of error**. A qualified human must always review and verify every output before any reliance or action. The agent assists; the human decides.
